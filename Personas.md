# Personas — Event Ticket Platform

## Product Context
Event organizers need a more convenient ticket-selling system so visitors can buy tickets directly (without intermediaries), receive QR codes, and attend events smoothly.

## Persona 1: Eglė — Independent Event Organizer

**Profile**
- Age: 31
- Role: Event Organizer (small to mid-size events)
- Tech level: Intermediate
- Works with: 2–5 person team

**Background**
Eglė organizes workshops, local concerts, and community events. She currently uses spreadsheets and social media messages to manage registrations and payments, which is slow and error-prone.

**Primary Goals**
- Publish event details quickly (title, description, date, location, price).
- Sell tickets in one place without third-party agencies.
- Automatically issue QR tickets after payment.
- Track ticket sales in real time.

**Pain Points**
- Manual ticket confirmation takes too much time.
- High platform fees reduce profit margins.
- Last-minute attendee questions create operational chaos.
- Hard to validate tickets quickly at the venue.

**Needs from the Platform**
- Easy event creation form with clear event catalog visibility.
- Fast checkout flow for attendees.
- Automatic QR code generation and email delivery.
- Organizer dashboard with sales and check-in status.

**Success Criteria**
- Event setup takes less than 15 minutes.
- At least 80% of tickets are sold directly via the platform.
- Check-in queues are reduced at event start.

## Persona 2: Lukas — Busy Attendee

**Profile**
- Age: 24
- Role: Customer / Event Visitor
- Tech level: High
- Device preference: Mobile-first

**Background**
Lukas attends concerts and tech meetups several times per month. He often decides quickly and expects a frictionless purchase flow on his phone.

**Primary Goals**
- Discover events quickly using search and filters.
- Buy tickets in under 2 minutes.
- Receive ticket and QR code instantly.
- Get reminders so he does not miss events.

**Pain Points**
- Too many steps in checkout cause drop-off.
- Tickets hidden in spam or sent late.
- Difficult event discovery when filters are weak.
- Stress at entrance if ticket cannot be found quickly.

**Needs from the Platform**
- Clean event catalog with date/category/location filters.
- Mobile-optimized checkout experience.
- Instant QR code delivery to email (and ideally wallet download).
- Automatic reminder notifications before event start.

**Success Criteria**
- Ticket purchase is completed in 2 minutes or less.
- Ticket is accessible in under 10 seconds at venue entrance.
- Reminder is received at least 24 hours before event.

---

## Persona 3: Ieva — Platform Administrator

**Profile**
- Age: 36
- Role: System Admin / Operations Manager
- Tech level: High
- Responsibility: Platform quality, trust, and compliance

**Background**
Ieva oversees daily platform operations, supports organizers, and ensures the system remains reliable during high-traffic periods.

**Primary Goals**
- Monitor platform health and incidents.
- Manage user and organizer accounts.
- Resolve disputes (payments, refunds, invalid scans).
- Maintain secure and stable ticketing operations.

**Pain Points**
- Lack of centralized monitoring delays response time.
- Fraud attempts and account misuse require quick action.
- Manual support workflows are hard to scale.
- Difficult to audit what happened during incidents.

**Needs from the Platform**
- Admin dashboard for users, events, and ticket activity.
- Permission-based access and audit logs.
- Alerts for abnormal behavior (e.g., rapid failed scans).
- Exportable reports for operations and decision-making.

**Success Criteria**
- Critical issues are detected and triaged quickly.
- Support ticket resolution time is reduced.
- Platform uptime and event-day stability remain high.

## Persona 4: Aldona — Low-Tech Attendee
 
**Profile**
- Age: 68
- Role: Casual Event Attendee
- Tech level: Low
- Device preference: Shared family tablet or desktop
 
**Background**
Aldona occasionally attends cultural events, choir concerts, and family celebrations. She is not confident with technology and relies on her daughter or grandson for help with anything beyond basic browsing. She has used online banking but finds unfamiliar websites confusing and stressful.
 
**Primary Goals**
- Find and purchase a ticket for a specific event she has already heard about.
- Receive the QR code ticket by email so she can access it when needed.
- Attend the event without confusion at the entrance.
 
**Pain Points**
- Unclear or cluttered interfaces cause her to abandon the process mid-way.
- Too many steps or unexpected redirects feel untrustworthy.
- Error messages are confusing and she does not know how to recover.
- Reminders are easy to miss if they are not clearly worded.
 
**Needs from the Platform**
- Simple, uncluttered event catalog with large readable text.
- Minimal checkout steps with clear progress indicators.
- QR code delivered by email with plain-language instructions on how to use it.
- Reminder notification before the event written in simple, direct language.
 
**Success Criteria**
- She completes a ticket purchase independently without calling for help.
- She is able to present her QR code at the venue entrance without assistance.
- She feels confident the purchase went through (clear confirmation screen and email).

## Persona 5: Dylan — Corporate Event Buyer
 
**Profile**
- Age: 34
- Role: HR Manager / Corporate Buyer
- Tech level: High
- Device preference: Desktop
 
**Background**
Dylan organizes team-building activities, conference visits, and professional development outings for his company's employees. He frequently needs to buy tickets in bulk, ensure expenses are properly documented for his accounting department, and distribute individual tickets to his colleagues without creating a massive email headache.
 
**Primary Goals**
- Buy multiple tickets at once in a single transaction.
- Receive an automated, company-compliant VAT invoice.
- Easily distribute individual QR code tickets to team members.
- Keep track of which employees have received their tickets.
 
**Pain Points**
- Buying tickets one-by-one is tedious and sometimes triggers fraud alerts on corporate credit cards.
- Getting a proper business invoice usually requires emailing the organizer and waiting days.
- Manually forwarding 20 different PDF tickets to 20 different coworkers is confusing and time-consuming.
 
**Needs from the Platform**
- A "buy in bulk" or group ticket option during the checkout flow.
- A form to enter company details for automatic invoice generation.
- A feature to input a list of emails so the system sends individual QR tickets directly to the attendees.
 
**Success Criteria**
- Bulk purchase and invoice generation happen instantly and automatically.
- Team members receive their individual QR codes directly to their inboxes without Dylan having to forward anything manually.
